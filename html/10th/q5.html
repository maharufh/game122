Time Allowed: 3 Hours      Maximum Marks: 80

General Instructions:

  1. All questions are compulsory.
  2. The question paper consists of 38 questions divided into 5 Sections A, B, C, D and E.
  3. Section A comprises of 20 questions of 1 mark each.
  4. Section B comprises of 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  5. Section C comprises of 6 questions of 3 marks each.
  6. Section D comprises of 4 questions of 5 marks each.
  7. Section E comprises of 3 case-based integrated units of assessment (4 marks each) with sub-parts.
  8. There is no overall choice. However, internal choices have been provided in some questions.

SECTION A (20 × 1 = 20 Marks)

  1. If the roots of the equation ax² + bx + c = 0 are equal, then the discriminant is:
    (a) b² – 4ac > 0   (b) b² – 4ac = 0   (c) b² – 4ac < 0   (d) b² + 4ac = 0
  2. The quadratic polynomial whose zeroes are 1 + √2 and 1 – √2 is:
    (a) x² – 2x – 1   (b) x² – 2x + 1   (c) x² + 2x – 1   (d) x² + 2x + 1
  3. If α, β are roots of x² – px + q = 0, then α² + β² = ?
    (a) p² – 2q   (b) p² + 2q   (c) q² – 2p   (d) q² + 2p
  4. The number of terms in the AP 3, 7, 11, ..., 407 is:
    (a) 100   (b) 101   (c) 102   (d) 103
  5. In ΔABC, if AB = c, BC = a, AC = b and ∠B = 90°, then sin C = ?
    (a) b/c   (b) a/c   (c) c/a   (d) a/b
  6. The volume of a cylinder with radius 5 cm and height 7 cm is:
    (a) 550 cm³   (b) 440 cm³   (c) 350 cm³   (d) 175 cm³
  7. The lateral surface area of a cuboid 10 cm × 5 cm × 4 cm is:
    (a) 180 cm²   (b) 200 cm²   (c) 220 cm²   (d) 240 cm²
  8. The mode of the data 4, 6, 6, 7, 8, 8, 8, 9 is:
    (a) 6   (b) 7   (c) 8   (d) 9
  9. If a coin is tossed twice, probability of getting at least one head is:
    (a) 1/4   (b) 1/2   (c) 3/4   (d) 1
  10. The area of a rhombus with diagonals 8 cm and 6 cm is:
    (a) 24 cm²   (b) 48 cm²   (c) 12 cm²   (d) 36 cm²
  11. cosec θ = ?
    (a) 1/sin θ   (b) 1/cos θ   (c) 1/tan θ   (d) sin θ
  12. The centroid of a triangle divides each median in the ratio:
    (a) 1:2   (b) 2:1   (c) 1:1   (d) 3:1
  13. The number of common tangents to two non-intersecting circles is:
    (a) 2   (b) 3   (c) 4   (d) 0
  14. The distance between points (0,0) and (5,12) is:
    (a) 13   (b) 17   (c) 10   (d) 15
  15. The probability of getting a number greater than 4 on a die is:
    (a) 1/3   (b) 1/2   (c) 2/3   (d) 1/6
  16. If HCF(84, 144) = 12, then LCM(84, 144) = ?
    (a) 1008   (b) 1000   (c) 1016   (d) 1024
  17. tan θ cot θ = ?
    (a) 1   (b) 0   (c) tan θ   (d) cot θ
  18. The volume of a sphere of diameter 6 cm is:
    (a) 36π cm³   (b) 72π cm³   (c) 108π cm³   (d) 144π cm³
  19. The area of an equilateral triangle with side 8 cm is:
    (a) 16√3 cm²   (b) 32√3 cm²   (c) 24√3 cm²   (d) 8√3 cm²
  20. Assertion (A): The polynomial x² + 1 has no real zeroes. Reason (R): Discriminant is negative.
    (a) Both true, R explains A   (b) Both true, R not explain   (c) A true R false   (d) A false

SECTION B (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

  1. Form the quadratic polynomial whose zeroes are –3 and 4.
  2. Check if –1 is a zero of the polynomial 2x³ + 3x² – x – 2.
  3. Solve the pair: 4x + 5y = 14 and 3x – 2y = 1.
    OR Show that the equations 2x + 3y = 6 and 4x + 6y = 12 have no solution.
  4. If sum of first n terms of an AP is 2n² + 3n, find the 10th term.
  5. If tan θ = 12/5, find sin θ and cos θ.

SECTION C (6 × 3 = 18 Marks)

  1. Solve 3x² – 5x + 2 = 0 using quadratic formula.
  2. A man is standing on the top of a building 50 m high. He observes a car moving away from the building. The angle of depression changes from 45° to 30° when the car moves 50 m away. Find the distance of the car from the building when angle is 30°.
  3. Prove the identity: cos²θ + sin²θ / cos²θ + sin²θ = 1.
  4. Find the point which divides the line segment joining (–2,4) and (6,8) externally in ratio 3:1.
  5. A metallic cube of edge 12 cm is melted and formed into a cylinder of radius 6 cm. Find the height of the cylinder.
  6. The following table shows the weights of 50 students. Find the mean weight using direct method.
    Weight (kg)40-5050-6060-7070-8080-90
    Frequency81215105

SECTION D (4 × 5 = 20 Marks)

  1. A person can row a boat at 5 km/h in still water. He takes thrice as much time to row upstream as to row downstream the same distance. Find the speed of the stream.
  2. A tent is in the shape of a cylinder surmounted by a cone. The diameter of the base is 28 m and height of cylinder is 10 m. The slant height of the cone is 17 m. Find the cost of canvas at ₹150 per m².
  3. Prove that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.
    OR Construct a right-angled triangle with hypotenuse 5 cm and one leg 3 cm.
  4. The table shows the marks obtained by 70 students in a test. Draw a 'less than' ogive and find the median marks.
    Marks0-1010-2020-3030-4040-5050-60
    Number of students5102015128

    Steps for drawing less than ogive and finding median to be followed.

SECTION E (3 × 4 = 12 Marks)

36. Profit Percentage (Quadratic Equations)
A shopkeeper buys an article for ₹x and sells it for ₹(x + 300), making 20% profit. If he had sold it for ₹(x + 500), he would have made 25% profit. Find the cost price.

(i) Form the quadratic equation. (1)
(ii) Solve for x. (1)
(iii) Find selling prices. (1)
(iv) Verify profits. (1)
37. Parabola Application (Coordinate Geometry)
The path of a projectile is given by y = x – (x²/100). A wall is at x = 50.

(i) Find height at x = 20. (1)
(ii) Find maximum height. (1)
(iii) Does it clear a 20 m wall at x = 50? (1)
(iv) Find range. (1)
38. Survey Data (Statistics + Probability)
In a survey of 100 families, number of children:
0(20), 1(30), 2(25), 3(15), 4(10)

(i) Find mean number of children. (1)
(ii) Find median. (1)
(iii) Find mode. (1)
(iv) Probability a randomly selected family has at least 2 children. (1)
