SECTION B (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

  1. Find a quadratic polynomial whose sum and product of zeroes are –5 and 6 respectively.
  2. Divide 3x³ + x² + 2x + 5 by x² – 2x + 1 and find quotient and remainder.
  3. Solve: 5x + 3y = 19 and 2x – 5y = –11.
    OR Find whether 4x + 5y = 20 and 8x + 10y = 40 are consistent.
  4. If the nth term of an AP is 4n – 3, find the 15th term and common difference.
  5. In a right triangle, if hypotenuse is 13 cm and one side is 5 cm, find the other side.

SECTION C (6 × 3 = 18 Marks)

  1. Using quadratic formula, solve x² – (√3 + 1)x + √3 = 0.
  2. A person standing on a bank of river observes the angle of elevation of top of a tree on opposite bank is 60°. On moving 20 m away, angle becomes 30°. Find height of tree.
  3. Prove that sec²θ – tan²θ = 1.
  4. Find the coordinates of the point which divides the line segment joining A(–1,3) and B(5,–9) in ratio 2:3 internally.
  5. A cylindrical vessel of radius 7 cm is filled with water. Find volume of water.
  6. The marks obtained by 30 students are given. Convert to grouped data and find mean using step deviation method.
    (Assume suitable ungrouped data in question)

SECTION D (4 × 5 = 20 Marks)

  1. Places A and B are 100 km apart on a highway. One car starts from A and another from B at same time. If they move in same direction, meet in 10 hours; if opposite direction, meet in 2 hours. Find their speeds.
  2. A solid iron rod of diameter 14 cm and length 20 cm is melted and recast into spheres of radius 3.5 cm. Find number of spheres.
  3. Prove that if a line divides any two sides of a triangle proportionally, then it is parallel to the third side (Converse of BPT).
    OR Construct a triangle with sides 5 cm, 6 cm, 7 cm and then another similar triangle whose sides are 7/5 of the first.
  4. The following distribution gives the heights (in cm) of 80 students in a class. Draw a 'more than' ogive for the data and hence find the median height.

    Height (more than or equal to)100110120130140150
    Cumulative Frequency80765836166

    To draw the more than ogive:
    - Take the upper class limits (100, 110, 120, 130, 140, 150) on the x-axis.
    - Take the corresponding more than cumulative frequencies (80, 76, 58, 36, 16, 6) on the y-axis.
    - Plot the points and join them with a free hand smooth curve.
    - To find median: Locate N/2 = 40 on the y-axis, draw a horizontal line to meet the curve, then drop a perpendicular to the x-axis. The point where it meets the x-axis is the median height.

Sum: 2(2), 3(4), 4(6), 5(8), 6(10), 7(12), 8(5), 9(2), 10(1)

(i) Find experimental probability of sum 7. (1)
(ii) Find experimental probability of sum >9. (1)
