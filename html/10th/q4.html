Time Allowed: 3 Hours      Maximum Marks: 80

General Instructions:

  1. All questions are compulsory.
  2. The question paper consists of 38 questions divided into 5 Sections A, B, C, D and E.
  3. Section A comprises of 20 questions of 1 mark each.
  4. Section B comprises of 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  5. Section C comprises of 6 questions of 3 marks each.
  6. Section D comprises of 4 questions of 5 marks each.
  7. Section E comprises of 3 case-based integrated units of assessment (4 marks each) with sub-parts.
  8. There is no overall choice. However, internal choices have been provided in some questions.

SECTION A (20 × 1 = 20 Marks)

  1. If p(x) = x² – 4x + 3, then p(3) = ?
    (a) 0   (b) 3   (c) 6   (d) 9
  2. The number of zeroes of the polynomial whose graph touches x-axis at one point and crosses at another is:
    (a) 1   (b) 2   (c) 3   (d) 0
  3. The condition for consistent pair of linear equations (intersecting lines) is:
    (a) a1/a2 = b1/b2 ≠ c1/c2   (b) a1/a2 ≠ b1/b2   (c) a1/a2 = b1/b2 = c1/c2   (d) a1/a2 = b1/b2 ≠ c1/c2
  4. The sum of an AP with first term 10, last term 40 and number of terms 7 is:
    (a) 175   (b) 245   (c) 210   (d) 280
  5. If cos θ = 5/13, then sin θ = ?
    (a) 12/13   (b) –12/13   (c) 5/12   (d) 13/12
  6. The total surface area of a hemisphere of radius 7 cm is:
    (a) 154 cm²   (b) 308 cm²   (c) 462 cm²   (d) 231 cm²
  7. The slant height of a cone with radius 8 cm and height 6 cm is:
    (a) 10 cm   (b) 14 cm   (c) 12 cm   (d) 16 cm
  8. The median of data 12, 15, 18, 20, 22, 25, 28 is:
    (a) 20   (b) 22   (c) 18   (d) 25
  9. If two dice are thrown, probability of getting sum 9 is:
    (a) 1/9   (b) 4/36   (c) 5/36   (d) 6/36
  10. The area of an isosceles triangle with sides 5 cm, 5 cm and base 6 cm is:
    (a) 12 cm²   (b) 15 cm²   (c) 10 cm²   (d) 20 cm²
  11. tan(45° + θ) = ?
    (a) (1 + tan θ)/(1 – tan θ)   (b) (1 – tan θ)/(1 + tan θ)   (c) tan θ   (d) 1
  12. The section formula for external division in ratio 1:1 is:
    (a) Midpoint   (b) Different   (c) Same   (d) None
  13. The number of tangents from a point inside the circle is:
    (a) 0   (b) 1   (c) 2   (d) infinite
  14. The distance from origin to point (3,4) is:
    (a) 5   (b) 7   (c) 3   (d) 4
  15. The probability of a sure event is:
    (a) 0   (b) 1   (c) 0.5   (d) undefined
  16. If HCF(96, 404) = 4, then LCM(96, 404) = ?
    (a) 9696   (b) 9700   (c) 9600   (d) 9800
  17. sec θ = ?
    (a) 1/cos θ   (b) 1/sin θ   (c) 1/tan θ   (d) cos θ
  18. The volume of a cuboid 10 cm × 5 cm × 4 cm is:
    (a) 200 cm³   (b) 100 cm³   (c) 150 cm³   (d) 300 cm³
  19. The area of a triangle with base 10 cm and height 5 cm is:
    (a) 25 cm²   (b) 50 cm²   (c) 20 cm²   (d) 30 cm²
  20. Assertion (A): (sin θ + cos θ)² + (sin θ – cos θ)² = 2. Reason (R): a² + b² + 2ab + a² + b² – 2ab = 2(a² + b²).
    (a) Both true, R explains A   (b) Both true, R not explain   (c) A true R false   (d) A false

SECTION B (5 × 2 = 10 Marks)

  1. Find the quadratic polynomial whose zeroes are 4 and –1.
  2. Verify whether 2 and –3 are zeroes of the polynomial x² + x – 6.
  3. Solve the system: x + y = 7 and x – y = 3.
    OR Determine if the equations 3x + 2y = 6 and 6x + 4y = 12 have solutions.
  4. The first term of an AP is 5 and the last term is 45. If sum is 400, find number of terms and common difference.
  5. In a right-angled triangle, if one acute angle is 30°, find the other angles and sides if hypotenuse is 10 cm.

SECTION C (6 × 3 = 18 Marks)

  1. Solve the quadratic equation 2x² – 7x + 3 = 0 by completing the square method.
  2. A statue 1.6 m tall casts a 3.2 m shadow. At the same time, a pole casts a shadow 6 m long. Find the height of the pole.
  3. Prove the identity: tan²θ + cot²θ + 2 = sec²θ cosec²θ.
  4. Find the area of the quadrilateral with vertices (1,1), (6,1), (6,6), (1,6).
  5. A cone of height 24 cm and radius 6 cm is cut into two parts by a plane parallel to base at height 8 cm from vertex. Find volume of smaller cone.
  6. The following data shows the marks of 40 students. Find the mean using assumed mean method.

SECTION D (4 × 5 = 20 Marks)

  1. A motor boat whose speed is 18 km/h in still water takes 1 hour more to go 24 km upstream than to return downstream to the same spot. Find the speed of the stream.

  3. Prove Pythagoras theorem using similar triangles.
    OR Draw a circle of radius 3 cm. From a point 7 cm away from centre, construct a pair of tangents.

SECTION E (3 × 4 = 12 Marks)

36. Speed Word Problem (Quadratic Equations)
A person travels 600 km partly by train and partly by car. If he covers 400 km by train and rest by car, it takes 5 hours. If he covers 200 km by train and rest by car, it takes 4 hours 20 minutes. Find speeds of train and car.

(i) Form the quadratic equation. (1)
(ii) Solve for the speeds. (1)
(iii) Verify one condition. (1)
(iv) Find time if entire journey by train. (1)
37. Coordinate Geometry Application
Points A(2,3), B(4,5), C(7,2) form a triangle. D is midpoint of BC.

(i) Find coordinates of D. (1)
(ii) Find length of AD. (1)
(iii) Find area of triangle ABC. (1)
(iv) Check if AD is median and find its length again. (1)
38. Card Probability (Probability)
A bag contains 6 red, 8 black and 4 white balls. Three balls are drawn at random without replacement.

(i) Find probability all three are red. (1)
(ii) Find probability one of each colour. (1)
(iii) Find probability at least two black. (1)
(iv) Find probability no white ball. (1)
