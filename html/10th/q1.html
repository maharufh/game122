CBSE Class 10 Mathematics (Standard)

Sample Question Paper 2025-26

Time Allowed: 3 Hours      Maximum Marks: 80

General Instructions:

  1. All questions are compulsory.
  2. The question paper consists of 38 questions divided into 5 Sections A, B, C, D and E.
  3. Section A comprises of 20 questions of 1 mark each.
  4. Section B comprises of 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  5. Section C comprises of 6 questions of 3 marks each.
  6. Section D comprises of 4 questions of 5 marks each.
  7. Section E comprises of 3 case-based integrated units of assessment (4 marks each) with sub-parts.
  8. There is no overall choice. However, internal choices have been provided in some questions.

SECTION A

(20 Questions × 1 Mark = 20 Marks)
Questions 1 to 20 are Multiple Choice Questions. Each question carries 1 mark.

  1. The HCF of 135 and 225 is:
    (a) 45   (b) 15   (c) 75   (d) 30
  2. Which of the following is an irrational number?
    (a) √16   (b) √25   (c) √36   (d) √2
  3. The discriminant of the quadratic equation x² – 4x + 4 = 0 is:
    (a) 0   (b) 16   (c) –16   (d) 8
  4. If the sum of first n terms of an AP is S_n = 3n² + 2n, then the common difference is:
    (a) 6   (b) 3   (c) 4   (d) 2
  5. In ΔABC, if DE ∥ BC, then by Basic Proportionality Theorem:
    (a) AD/DB = AE/EC   (b) AD/AE = DB/EC   (c) AB/AC = DE/BC   (d) None
  6. The value of sin²θ + cos²θ is:
    (a) 0   (b) 1   (c) 2   (d) –1
  7. The length of tangent from a point 25 cm away from the centre of a circle of radius 7 cm is:
    (a) 24 cm   (b) 18 cm   (c) 7 cm   (d) 25 cm
  8. Area of a sector with central angle 90° in a circle of radius 14 cm is:
    (a) 154 cm²   (b) 77 cm²   (c) 308 cm²   (d) 44 cm²
  9. The mean of data: 10, 15, 20, 25, 30 is:
    (a) 20   (b) 15   (c) 25   (d) 18
  10. Empirical probability of getting head if in 50 tosses, 28 heads:
    (a) 28/50   (b) 22/50   (c) 50/28   (d) 1
  11. If p(x) = x² + 3x + 2, then p(-2) = ?
    (a) 0   (b) -4   (c) 4   (d) 8
  12. The pair of linear equations 3x + 2y = 5 and 2x – 3y = 7 has:
    (a) One solution   (b) Two solutions   (c) No solution   (d) Infinitely many
  13. The distance between points (1,2) and (-1, -2) is:
    (a) 4√2   (b) 4   (c) 2√2   (d) 8
  14. sin 60° = ?
    (a) 1/2   (b) √3/2   (c) √2/2   (d) 1
  15. The area of an equilateral triangle with side 4 cm is:
    (a) 4√3 cm²   (b) 2√3 cm²   (c) √3 cm²   (d) 8√3 cm²
  16. In a circle, the angle subtended by an arc at the centre is 120°. The major sector area (radius 21 cm) is:
    (a) 462 cm²   (b) 231 cm²   (c) 693 cm²   (d) 924 cm²
  17. The mode of data 5, 7, 7, 8, 9, 9, 9, 10 is:
    (a) 7   (b) 9   (c) 8   (d) 10
  18. If P(E) = 0.3, then P(not E) = ?
    (a) 0.7   (b) 0.3   (c) 1   (d) 0
  19. The zeroes of quadratic polynomial x² - 5x + 6 are:
    (a) 2,3   (b) -2,-3   (c) 1,6   (d) -1,-6
  20. Assertion (A): √2 is irrational. Reason (R): A number whose prime factors are not perfect squares is irrational.
    (a) Both A and R true, R explains A   (b) Both true, R not explain   (c) A true R false   (d) A false R true

SECTION B

(5 Questions × 2 Marks = 10 Marks)

  1. Prove that √5 is irrational.
    OR Prove that 3 + 2√5 is irrational.
  2. Find the zeroes of the polynomial x² – 3x – 10.
  3. Solve the pair of linear equations: 2x + 3y = 12 and x – y = 1.
  4. Find the 20th term of the AP: 5, 9, 13, … .
  5. In a triangle, the angles are in the ratio 2:3:4. Find the measure of each angle.

SECTION C

(6 Questions × 3 Marks = 18 Marks)

  1. Prove the Basic Proportionality Theorem (Thales' theorem).
  2. Find the value of k for which the quadratic equation x² + kx + 9 = 0 has equal roots.
  3. A ladder 15 m long reaches a window 12 m high from the ground. Find the distance of the foot of the ladder from the wall.
  4. Prove the identity: 1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ.
  5. Find the area of the triangle with vertices at (1,1), (2,3) and (3,1).
  6. The following frequency distribution shows the marks obtained by 50 students in a test. Find the mode.
    Marks0-1010-2020-3030-4040-50
    Number of students51218105

SECTION D

(4 Questions × 5 Marks = 20 Marks)

  1. Solve graphically the equations: 2x + y = 6 and 4x – 2y = 4. Also find the area of the triangle formed by the lines and x-axis.
  2. A train travels 360 km at a uniform speed. If the speed had been 5 km/h more, it would have taken 1 hour less. Find the original speed.
  3. Prove that the lengths of tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal.
    OR Construct a triangle similar to a given triangle with scale factor 3/4.
  4. The following table shows marks of students. Find mean, median and mode.
    Marks0-1010-2020-3030-4040-50
    No. of students5812105

SECTION E

(3 Case-Based Questions × 4 Marks = 12 Marks)

36. Human Pyramid in Sports Event (Quadratic Equations)
A school is forming a human pyramid with odd number of students per row: top row 1, then 3, 5, and so on. They have 45 students.

(i) Write the quadratic equation for total students in n rows. (1)
(ii) Solve to find maximum n rows possible exactly with 45 students. (1)
(iii) How many additional students needed for one more row? (1)
(iv) Find discriminant and nature of roots. (1)
37. Circular Park (Coordinate Geometry + Circles)
A circular park has centre O(0,0) and radius 50 m. Gates A(30,40) and B(-40,30). Visitor at P(100,0).

(i) Verify if A and B lie on the circle. (1)
(ii) Find distance between A and B. (1)
(iii) Find length of tangent from P to the circle. (1)
(iv) Find the equation of chord AB. (1)
38. Study Hours Survey (Statistics + Probability)
Survey of 40 students on daily study hours:
Hours0-22-44-66-88-10
Students4812106


(i) Identify the modal class. (1)
(ii) Calculate mean study hours (use direct method). (1)
(iii) Probability a random student studies 8-10 hours given >6 hours. (1)
(iv) Probability a student studies at most 4 hours. (1)
